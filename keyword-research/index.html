The Keyword Research Process

Every keyword research project I deliver follows a rigorous, systematic process designed to maximize strategic accuracy and actionable insight. Here is a breakdown of the methodology I use from brief to final deliverable:

Phase 1: Discovery & Seed Identification

Before analyzing a single metric, I conduct deep discovery into your industry, business model, target audience, and competitive landscape. This includes:

  • Seed keyword brainstorming â€” collaborating with you to identify core topics, product categories, and service areas that define your business.

  • Customer language mapping â€” analyzing how your actual customers describe your products/services in reviews, forums, and social media to identify natural language variations.
  • Industry trend analysis â€” using Google Trends and search demand curves to identify emerging topics and seasonal patterns in your niche.

Phase 2: Search Intent Classification

Every keyword has a specific user intent behind it. I classify each keyword into one of four intent categories:

  4. Transactional: Users ready to take immediate action (e.g., "hire keyword researcher"). Optimized for conversion pages and direct CTAs.

Modern SEO is entity-driven. I organize keywords into semantic clusters built around core entities:

  • Cluster architecture â€” mapping supporting sub-topics around each pillar to create a comprehensive topical hierarchy.

  • Knowledge Graph alignment â€” ensuring your entity relationships mirror those in Google's Knowledge Graph for maximum semantic relevance.

AEO & GEO Keyword Strategy

In 2026, traditional SEO keyword research alone is not enough. Your keyword strategy must also account for Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) to capture visibility across all discovery channels.

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) Keywords

AEO keyword research identifies the natural language queries that AI-powered answer engines â€” such as Google's Search Generative Experience (SGE) â€” extract as definitive answers. My AEO keyword process includes:

  • Identifying question-format queries (who, what, how, why) that trigger featured snippets and AI overviews.
  • Mapping declarative answer patterns â€” the exact sentence structures AI engines prefer for extraction.
  • Targeting "People Also Ask" expansions to capture cascading query chains and increase SERP real estate.
  • Analyzing Speakable schema opportunities for voice search optimization.

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) Keywords

GEO keyword research focuses on making your content the preferred citation source for large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity. My GEO keyword strategies include:

  • Identifying citation-worthy query patterns â€” the types of questions where LLMs need to reference external sources.
  • Targeting data-rich, statistic-driven keywords that AI models cannot answer from training data alone.
  • Building entity authority through consistent, schema-marked content across multiple pages.
  • Mapping conversational query chains that users input in AI chat interfaces.

VEO & SXO Keyword Integration

Voice Engine Optimization (VEO) and Search Experience Optimization (SXO) complement AEO and GEO by ensuring your keyword strategy covers conversational voice queries and aligns with user experience signals. Keywords I identify include natural language phrases for voice assistants (VEO) and terms that correlate with high engagement metrics like time-on-page, low bounce rates, and clear conversion pathways (SXO).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is semantic keyword research?

Semantic keyword research is the process of identifying keywords based on their contextual meaning, entity relationships, and topical relevance rather than just search volume. It involves mapping how search engines and AI models understand the relationships between concepts, ensuring your content covers an entire topic comprehensively to build topical authority.

How is keyword research different for AEO and GEO?

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) keyword research focuses on identifying natural language questions and declarative statement patterns that AI models like ChatGPT, Google SGE, and Perplexity extract as definitive answers. This goes beyond traditional volume metrics to focus on "answerable" intent and citation-worthy query patterns.

What is entity-based keyword clustering?

Entity-based keyword clustering groups keywords around core entities (people, concepts, products, locations) rather than simple string similarity. This approach mirrors how Google's Knowledge Graph and AI language models understand topics, resulting in content strategies that achieve broader topical coverage and stronger ranking signals.

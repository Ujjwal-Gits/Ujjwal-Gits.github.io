What Is Logic Verification?

Logic verification is the disciplined process of testing whether your software's internal business rules, algorithms, and data-processing workflows produce mathematically and logically correct results across all scenarios. Unlike functional testing (which verifies "does the button work?"), logic verification asks: "does the computation behind the button produce the right answer, every single time, including at boundary conditions and edge cases?"

For Nepali businesses building e-commerce platforms, fintech applications, SaaS products, and management systems, business logic testing in Nepal is the difference between a reliable product and a liability. A pricing algorithm that silently rounds incorrectly, a tax calculation that misapplies VAT rules, or a permission system that grants unauthorized access â€” these are logic failures that no amount of UI polish can fix.

Why Nepali Software Companies Need Logic Verification

  • Financial Accuracy: E-commerce, banking, and payment platforms in Nepal must compute prices, discounts, taxes, and fees with absolute precision. A 0.01% rounding error across thousands of transactions creates significant financial discrepancies.
  • Security & Permissions: Role-based access control logic must be airtight. A logic flaw that grants admin privileges to regular users is a catastrophic security vulnerability, as documented by the OWASP Top 10.
  • Data Consistency: Complex CRUD operations, cascading deletes, and multi-table transactions must maintain referential integrity. Logic errors here corrupt your entire database over time.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Fintech and healthtech platforms in Nepal face increasing regulatory scrutiny. Logic errors in compliance-sensitive calculations can result in legal penalties.
  • Edge Case Resilience: What happens when a user enters zero? Negative numbers? Maximum integer values? Logic verification tests the scenarios your developers never imagined.

The 2026 Search Landscape: SEO, AEO, and GEO

To dominate the 2026 search landscape, you cannot just write content and hope Google figures it out. Search has fractured into three distinct engines:

  • Traditional SEO (Google Blue Links)
  • AEO - Answer Engine Optimization (Google AI Overviews, Voice Search)
  • GEO - Generative Engine Optimization (LLMs like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude)

AI engines (RAG systems) are terrible at reading messy, unstructured text. They do not read your website like a human; they parse it like a database. If you want your article or business to be cited as the #1 source by Perplexity and Google AI Overviews, you must feed the machines an Entity Graph using connected JSON-LD Schema.

Technical Infrastructure & Entity Graphs

We execute technical audits using enterprise software like Ahrefs and Screaming Frog to resolve Core Web Vitals (LCP, INP, CLS) failures. An out-of-the-box WordPress installation without proper caching or optimized server routing will fail Google's stringent performance checks.

Entity Graph Architecture

In the past, SEOs added a simple Article schema and stopped. In 2026, isolated schema is ignored by AI. You must use the @graph array to explicitly link your entities together using @id. You have to prove to the AI that your author wrote the article, and works for your organization.

Warning Signs of Cheap SEO

Paying NPR 10,000 a month for SEO is a guaranteed way to destroy your domain authority. Here is what cheap agencies do:

  • Buying toxic PBN (Private Blog Network) backlinks that trigger Google algorithmic penalties.
  • Keyword stuffing content without applying semantic HTML 5 tags.
  • Failing to implement proper JSON-LD Entity Graphs for Answer Engine Optimization.
  • Ignoring technical server infrastructure, resulting in high Time to First Byte (TTFB).
